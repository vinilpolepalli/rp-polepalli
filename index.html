Effects of Epigallocatechin Gallate (EGCG) and Piperine Compounds on the Regeneration of D. Tigrina

Background

This research project tested the potential of Epigallocatechin Gallate (EGCG) & Piperine compounds on the Regeneration of Dugesia Tigrina (Planaria) to see if they can be utilized as a potential cancer treatment together.

EGCG is most abundant antioxidant and catechin in green tea. Recent studies have revealed that EGCG effectively inhibits tumor growth by targeting proteins such as vascular endothelial growth factor and epidermal growth factor receptor, which are crucial for angiogenesis and cell proliferation.

Piperine is an abundant alkaloid in black pepper. Piperine has been shown to modulate the WNT B-catenin pathway implicated with key processes like gene transcription and cell regulation. Additionally, piperine has been shown to enhance the bioavailability of co-administered compounds by inhibiting enzymes like cytochrome P450, thus improving nutrient and drug absorption.

Dugesia Tigrina, more commonly known as planaria, was used as the test subject for the experiment. Planaria is a flatworm that regenerates its entire body from a tiny fragment. This ability to regenerate makes them a valuable model organism for studying tissue regeneration processes, the roles of stem cells, growth factors, and signaling pathways in the regeneration process. Furthermore, the planarian stem cells share signaling pathways used in cancer cells, like the Wnt, Hedgehog, and Notch pathways.

Rationale

Cancer treatments have been a major focus of the medical field for decades. Some common methods that combat cancer include radiation, chemotherapy, and hormonal therapy (Warren et al., 2008). However, despite undergoing rigorous clinical trials, many of these treatments still have flaws. In 2019, the survival rate for individuals diagnosed with various cancers between 0 and < 10 years after diagnosis was approximately 55% (Warren et al., 2008). In addition, 49% of cancer patients experienced financial hardship (Smith et al., 2019). Today, there is a need for a highly effective and affordable treatment. Compounds such as epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG) and piperine have shown promise as potential cancer treatments that fit these constraints (Smith et al., 2019).

Hypothesis

As a result of the medicinal properties of the two compounds, it was hypothesized that planaria treated with various concentrations of EGCG and Piperine over a 7-day period will regenerate at a slower rate compared to planaria grown in regular spring water.
